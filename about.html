ABOUT

The Story

Twenty years of drinking wine and talking about it. I started writing about wine in the early 2000s, back when wine blogs were new and wine snobbery was still the norm. My view then was the same as it is now: great wine should be for everyone, and the best thing you can do for someone is point them at a bottle they'd never have found on their own. I spent twenty years working in retail — not always in wine, but that became my love and after taking a degree in wine led me to opening The Wine Twit on Nightingale Lane in 2016. The name says everything about the approach: Whilst i think I can prove to you taht I know what works with wine at the hert of it , I'm a wine twit who loves wine and wants to share it. We're not a hushed temple to the grape. We're a neighbourhood shop. You tell me what you like or what you're cooking tonight, or even who you're buying for, and I'll find you something brilliant. That's it.

The Range

The shop stocks 120 to 180 bottles at any one time. Every one of them is something I'd genuinely recommend to a friend. We lean towards smaller producers, interesting regions, and bottles that over-deliver on their price. You'll find classic French alongside Georgian orange wine, Galician Albariño next to Jura Savagnin. If it's boring, it doesn't get a shelf.

On The Supper Club

The supper club started as an experiment and became the thing I'm most proud of. Chef Lawrence designs a four-course menu around the wines — not the other way round — and we squeeze as many people around the tables as the room will hold. The regulars keep coming back. That tells me everything I need to know.

On Bloody Bens

There's a gin distillery in the basement, run by our friends at Bloody Bens. If that's not a reason to visit, I don't know what is.
